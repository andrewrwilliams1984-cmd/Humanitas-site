Mission

Humanitas exists to help ensure that as the world advances, our humanity does not fall behind.

We are building a body of public work focused on human development in an age shaped by rapid technological change.

Why this matters

Technology is reshaping work, relationships, education, and daily life at extraordinary speed. The question is not only what becomes possible, but what kind of people and societies we are becoming in the process.

What Humanitas focuses on

Humanitas is focused on the qualities that make us deeply human: awareness, compassion, judgement, responsibility, connection, and the ability to act with wisdom.

What we are building toward

Our aim is to help make human development more visible, more practical, and more central to the systems shaping the future.

Principles

We believe progress should deepen our humanity, not diminish it.

Human development should not be a luxury.
Progress does not need to come at the cost of our humanity.
Technology should support human depth, not replace it.
What we build should help people become more aware, more connected, and more capable of wise action.
The conditions shaping human life should strengthen, not erode, our capacity for judgement, care, and responsibility.
A more advanced world should also be a more humane one.

What we do

Humanitas works across ideas, tools, and practical applications.

Our work is designed to make human development easier to understand, easier to embed, and harder to ignore.

Research and frameworks

We bring together ideas, evidence, and long-view thinking to help define what human development can look like in a rapidly changing world.

Measurement and accountability

We explore practical ways to assess whether systems are strengthening the qualities that make us more human, or weakening them.

Products and infrastructure

We are building tools, environments, and public-facing work that make human development more practical and more widely shared.

A simple idea

Human development should be part of the future by design.

Too often it is treated as something extra — a private interest, a wellness add-on, or something left to chance. We believe it belongs much closer to the centre.

Our direction

To help build a world where our humanity matters as much as capability.
